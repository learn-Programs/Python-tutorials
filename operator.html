Operators

Python supports both arithmetic and comparison operator, in this tutorial we will learn about these operators.

Operators are special symbols in Python that carry out arithmetic or logical computation. The value of the operator operates on is called the operand.
for example, x + y Here, + is the operator which performs the addition on x and y which are the operands..

Operator Meaning
+ Add two operands.
– Subtract two operands.
* Multiply two operands.
/ Divide two operands.
% Modulus – the remainder of the division two operands.
// Floor division- division that results in the whole number.
** Exponent- left operand raised to the power of right.
example.py

>>> 2 + 4
6

>>> 4 - 2
2

>>> 2 * 4
8
>>> 8 / 4
2.0

>>> 9 % 4
1

>>> 8 // 4
2

>>> 2 ** 2
4

Comparison Operators

Comparison operators are used to comparing values. it returns True or False according to the condition.

Operator Meaning
< less than
<= less than equal to
> greater than
>= greater than equal to
== equal to
!= not equal to
example.py

>>> x = 10
>>> y = 15

>>> x < y
True

>>> x > y
False

>>> x == y
False
